Clinical Interview Transcript - Patient Case Study

Patient: 45-year-old female
Chief Complaint: Chest pain and difficulty swallowing

History of Present Illness:
The patient reports a 3-month history of chest pain that occurs primarily when eating. She describes the pain as a tightness behind her sternum in the middle of her chest. The pain is worse when lying down and she has noticed that food gets stuck when she tries to swallow, particularly with solid foods. 

She reports current heartburn and reflux symptoms that have been present for several years. She mentions that antacids used to help with her symptoms but are no longer providing relief. The patient denies any pain that radiates to her neck, arms, or jaw.

The patient has experienced some weight loss over the past few months and reports decreased exercise tolerance. She denies any associated shortness of breath, cough, or hoarse voice.

Past Medical History:
- Hypertension, currently taking amlodipine
- No history of coronary artery disease, peripheral artery disease, or prior myocardial infarction
- No diabetes mellitus type 2
- No history of stroke

Social History:
- Prior tobacco use but quit 5 years ago
- Denies current tobacco use
- Denies alcohol use
- Recent social stress due to work changes

Family History:
- Father had myocardial infarction at age 60
- No family history of rheumatoid arthritis or cancer

Review of Systems:
- Denies fever, nausea, vomiting, or early satiety
- Denies palpitations or diaphoresis
- Denies joint swelling, morning stiffness, or rheumatoid nodules
- Denies hand thickness, finger ulcers, or Raynaud's phenomenon
- Denies rash or telangiectasias
- Denies dry eyes, red eyes, or vision changes
- Denies hemoptysis, hematemesis, or panic attacks
- Denies recent travel, procedures, or medication changes
- No recent injuries or gestational complications

Physical Examination:
- Middle-aged female in no acute distress
- Vital signs stable
- No neck masses or fullness noted
- Cardiovascular examination normal
- Pulmonary examination normal
- No joint deformities or swelling observed

Assessment and Plan:
Patient presents with chest pain and dysphagia concerning for possible gastroesophageal disorder. Further workup recommended including upper endoscopy and possible manometry studies.
